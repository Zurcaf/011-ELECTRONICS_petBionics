% ----------------------------------------------------------------------
\section{Planned Tasks}
\label{section:tasks}

The remaining work is structured into four phases, building on the functional
prototype and preliminary dataset described in Chapter~\ref{chapter:preliminary}.

\textbf{Phase~2 — Animal data collection.}
The primary objective of this phase is to acquire labelled locomotion data
from canine subjects fitted with the prototype.
This involves coordinating with a veterinary clinic or rehabilitation centre,
instrumenting the prosthesis on the target animal, and conducting structured
walking sessions under controlled and free-movement conditions.
Multiple sessions across different days are planned to capture variability
in adaptation state.
The acquired data will provide the ground-truth basis for subsequent
feature extraction and model training.

\textbf{Phase~3 — Signal processing and feature extraction.}
Raw CSV signals will be processed to extract the biomechanical features
described in Chapter~\ref{chapter:methodology}: force-related metrics
(peak load, load consistency), temporal metrics (stride duration, step
regularity, phase timing), and signal variability metrics (RMS variability,
inter-stride coefficient of variation).
Gait cycle segmentation will be performed using the event detector already
implemented in firmware, validated against manual annotation of the
acquired sessions.

\textbf{Phase~4 — Machine learning model development.}
The extracted feature vectors will be used to train and evaluate supervised
classification models for prosthesis adaptation state assessment.
A Random Forest classifier will be used as the primary model, with logistic
regression and support vector machines evaluated as baselines.
Model selection will be based on cross-validated classification performance
(accuracy, precision, recall, F1-score) and interpretability of the learned
feature importances.
Data augmentation and leave-one-session-out cross-validation will be used to
address the limited dataset size expected in this phase.

\textbf{Phase~5 — Validation, integration, and writing.}
The complete system will be validated end-to-end, from data acquisition to
adaptation state classification.
Longitudinal consistency of the predictions across multiple sessions will be
assessed to confirm that the model captures genuine adaptation trends rather
than session-to-session noise.
Concurrently, the dissertation document will be completed, incorporating all
experimental results and analysis.

\section{Risks and Contingency Plan}
\label{section:risks}

Table~\ref{tab:risks} identifies the main risks to the project schedule and
quality, together with their assessed likelihood and impact, and the
corresponding mitigation strategies.

\begin{table}[h]
  \centering
  \caption{Risk assessment and mitigation strategies.}
  \label{tab:risks}
  \begin{tabular}{p{3.8cm} c c p{5.8cm}}
    \toprule
    \textbf{Risk} & \textbf{Likelihood} & \textbf{Impact} & \textbf{Mitigation} \\
    \midrule
    Difficulty obtaining canine subjects for data collection
    & Medium & High
    & Establish partnership with a veterinary clinic early in Phase~2;
      if access is delayed, supplement with data from healthy dogs at
      kennels or shelters as a fallback. \\
    \addlinespace
    Insufficient labelled data for ML training
    & Medium & High
    & Apply leave-one-session-out cross-validation; explore data
      augmentation (time warping, noise injection); consider transfer
      learning from human prosthetics datasets as a prior. \\
    \addlinespace
    HX711 ADC noise under dynamic conditions
    & Low & Medium
    & Adaptive digital filtering already implemented in firmware;
      if noise remains problematic, replace with a higher-SNR 24-bit
      ADC (e.g.\ ADS1232) without changes to the software pipeline. \\
    \addlinespace
    Sensor displacement due to prosthetic fit variability
    & Medium & Medium
    & Design a mechanical fixture to enforce repeatable sensor
      positioning; use session-level normalisation to reduce
      sensitivity to absolute orientation. \\
    \addlinespace
    Battery autonomy insufficient for field sessions
    & Low & Low
    & Wi-Fi-off acquisition mode is already implemented, reducing
      current draw from $\approx$150\,mA to $\approx$45\,mA and
      extending autonomy beyond 12\,h; larger-capacity cells can
      replace the current 750\,mAh pack if needed. \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\end{table}
